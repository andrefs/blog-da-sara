Here comes the sun or white fish and shrimp on a tomato and feta dance

  • 1 tsp. of olive oil
  • 1\2 of big onion or 1 medium
  • 6 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 2 bay leaves, crushed

100ml of white wine 5 big tomatoes, diced or 250g-diced tomato can 4/6 white fish fillets cooked (reserve the water) 500g of shrimp (fresh or frozen) 200g of feta cheese or any similar hard milky cheese 1/2 of a lemon (optional) A splash of balsamic vinegar (optional) 1 dried chili, crumbled (those tiny ones) (optional) A handful of thyme 1 tbsp. of oregano Olives to taste Salt and pepper to taste

  1. Warm up a wok or a big pan, add the olive oil and let the onion, garlic and bay dance together for a little, until lightly golden (3m)
  2. Add the tomatoes or the tomatoes can, the wine, the oregano and the thyme, some water from cooking the fish and the dried chili. Season carefully and let it cook for 7-10m, until the sauce is thicker and a bit reduced.
  3. Add the fish and the shrimp, some lemon juice and the balsamic vinegar and cook for a couple minutes or until the shrimp is cooked, if using frozen.
  4. Taste and rectify the seasoning. Then take from the heat and sprinkle over the cheese, the olives and some more thyme leaves.
  5. Serve immediately with some bread or some simple basmati rice.

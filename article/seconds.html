Seconds

And there were times in my life where I think I should have stayed a little longer. Tried a little harder. Fake it a bit more, for the sake of responsibility. But not this time. There's hard and there's too hard. I've learned to tell the difference. No shame. No regrets. No looking back.

It's been two months since I've moved out of Berlin. I know it sounds strange since my first post was about beginning, my beginning in Berlin. I did try to love a world where the warmth of the sun was rare. But I guess people are right about love: you can't force it.

I've heard a lot of things ever since I've moved back to Portugal: "don't you feel a bit like a failure?” "couldn't you have tried more?", "you are really instable with your decisions", "don't you feel bad for leaving your boyfriend there?". Well, the cliché strikes again: you had to be in my shoes to know. It was not an easy choice. It's never an easy decision for me to give up on something that I initially believed it would make me happy(er). And there were times in my life where I think I should have stayed a little longer. Tried a little harder. Fake it a bit more, for the sake of responsibility. But not this time. There's hard and there's too hard. I've learned to tell the difference. No shame. No regrets. No looking back.

Now I'm back living in Porto, a city that I have not lived in for almost seven years. It feels good to be back. I see the city in a new light. Everything is pretty much the same, but I feel different. Like I have lived other lives. And I did. Oh, I really did.

Lisbon was my previous home and will always be the love of my life. It was there that I've become a woman, that I've challenged myself the most. Me and her, we grew together. I will always love her. The hilly paths. The hidden corners. That unbelievable light. How we danced together, how sweet and vibrant was our love. But love, by itself, it's never enough. It was time to leave.

I'm writing this entry from my grandmother’s house, from my old room. It's raining heavily outside, I can almost hear the rage of the tide. I'm not really sure of how my life will unfold from now on. I still don't know what I'll be when I grow up. The world is going through some really weird turns. But I have this sweet melody in my head.

Here's to the ones who dream.
